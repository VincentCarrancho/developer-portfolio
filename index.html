FIU COVID-19 App Prototype – Spring 2021

During the 2021 spring semester, I was assigned to lead a team of 12 people to develop a COVID-19 app using Flutter throughout a 9 week development period. Despite having almost no experience in Flutter (or any type of mobile development experience), we managed to create a working prototype.

The app we created was an app that served two purposes. The first one being to help students complete a safety check before they came on campus. The second purpose was to let people know if they were exposed to COVID-19 while on school grounds–unfortunately, we were not able to implement the bluetooth protocols during the development period. We used Firebase for our backend, Flutter as the framework to develop our app for both platforms of phones, and Figma to design the user interface.


The Social Distancing Graduation Cap – Spring 2020

Due to COVID-19, everyone’ morale was low. I saw an opportunity to make other laugh and enjoy themselves while riding out the pandemic. I wanted to make something so goofy that related to the situation we were in. Therefore, I decided to get creative with my graduation cap. What I did was that I attached an ultrasonic sensor, and Arduino, and a beeper on my graduation cap.

What the graduation cap did was that the ultrasonic sensor feeds distance readings into the Arduino. The Arduino processes the data from the sensor and performs calculations that controls the timing of the beeper. The end result was a graduation cap that starts beeping when it detects something is 6ft or closer–then beeps faster if the object does get closer.
